LEGENDARY

Newport Folk Festival

July 25, 1965 - Newport, Rhode Island

The performance that changed everything. Dylan plugged in an electric guitar and folk purists lost their minds. Pete Seeger allegedly tried to cut the power cables with an axe.

Forest Hills Tennis Stadium

August 28, 1965 - Queens, New York

One month after Newport. The booing continued, but Dylan pressed on. The electric half of the show was met with hostility from folk fans.

1966

The World Tour

Dylan and The Hawks face hostile audiences nightly

ICONIC

Manchester Free Trade Hall

May 17, 1966

After someone yells "Judas!", Dylan responds "I don't believe you... you're a liar" then tells the band to "Play it f***ing loud!"

Royal Albert Hall

May 26-27, 1966 - London

The final shows before Dylan's motorcycle accident. Some of his most intense performances ever captured.

Sydney Stadium

April 1966 - Australia

Part of the world tour. Australian audiences were somewhat more receptive than their British counterparts.

1970s

The Return

After years of silence, Dylan returns to touring

Tour '74 with The Band

January-February 1974

Dylan's first tour in 8 years. Arena rock treatment with The Band. Captured on "Before the Flood" album.

ESSENTIAL

Rolling Thunder Revue

1975-1976

Face-painted, theatrical performances. Completely reimagined arrangements. Perhaps the most passionate versions ever.

Hard Rain TV Special

May 1976 - Fort Collins, Colorado

Broadcast on NBC. Performed in pouring rain, hence the album title. Raw and unrelenting.

1980s

The Wilderness Years

Dylan reinvents himself constantly

Live Aid

July 13, 1985 - Philadelphia

With Keith Richards and Ronnie Wood. Famously shambolic performance at the global charity concert.

True Confessions Tour

1986 - with Tom Petty & The Heartbreakers

Dylan backed by one of rock's tightest bands. Energetic, polished performances.

1990s

The Never Ending Tour

Dylan begins touring relentlessly, 100+ shows per year

MTV Unplugged

November 1994 - Sony Studios, NYC

Acoustic arrangement for the famous TV series. Stripped back to basics.

Woodstock '94

August 1994 - Saugerties, NY

25 years after the original Woodstock (which Dylan famously skipped).

Never Ending Tour 1995

1995 - Various Venues

The song continued to evolve nightly on the endless road.

Love and Theft Tour

2001-2002

Dylan in his "crooner" phase, with blues-influenced arrangements.

Dylan's voice has changed dramatically, but the power remains.
