The Song That Changed Everything

"Like a Rolling Stone" is widely regarded as one of the most influential songs in rock history. At over six minutes long, it defied radio conventions of the era and proved that rock music could be intellectually demanding and commercially successful.

Dylan wrote the song in a marathon session after returning exhausted from a tour of England. What began as pages of stream-of-consciousness writing was distilled into four verses of bitter, liberating poetry.

The recording session on June 15-16, 1965 produced the iconic sound partly by accident - Al Kooper, primarily a guitarist, sat down at the organ despite not being a keyboard player, creating the swirling sound that defined the track.
